Medical Supply.
Delivered by Drone.

Aerovyn LLC is developing a lightweight, thermally insulated medical supply pod that mounts to drones already fielded by U.S. military squads, law enforcement, and industrial safety teams. When a soldier is down in a contact zone, a firefighter is trapped in a collapse, or a worker is injured miles from the nearest paramedic, sending another person in risks creating a second casualty. The AC-1 Aerovyn Capsule eliminates that problem by turning the drone already on scene into a medical resupply vehicle.

Our Mission

The gap between life
and death is access.

The capsule carries a full TCCC trauma loadout and works with any compatible drone via the ARM-1 mount bracket. It follows the military's standard MARCH trauma protocol, addressing hemorrhage, airway obstruction, and tension pneumothorax; the three causes that account for virtually all preventable pre-hospital deaths.

Product Suite

Two Products.
One System.

The Aerovyn system is a thermally managed payload capsule paired with a reusable mechanical mount bracket. The bracket stays on the drone. The capsule deploys with each mission. Zero new airframe required.

01 / Primary Payload
Aerovyn Capsule
AC-1 · Payload System

A single-use, thermally managed medical supply capsule pre-loaded with a full TECC/MARCH trauma loadout. Contents address the top three preventable causes of traumatic death: hemorrhage, airway obstruction, and tension pneumothorax. Opens clamshell with a single button press, one-handed, with gloves on.

Active Thermal TCCC / TECC Single-Use Blue UAS
02 / Interface Hardware
Recovery Mount
ARM-1 · Bracket System

A reusable mechanical bracket that interfaces the AC-1 with compatible drone platforms via a published attachment standard. The capsule locks in before flight and releases automatically on landing through a passive pressure trigger. The drone returns to the operator. No electronics interface required. One bracket stays on the drone indefinitely.

The ARM-1 bracket is a one-time acquisition per drone. Each mission consumes one AC-1 capsule. Agencies that equip their fleet create a predictable recurring resupply requirement on installed hardware.
